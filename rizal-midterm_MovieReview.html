RZL1000 Exam

Movie Review – Bayaning 3rd World

MIDTERM EXAMINATION IN RZL1000 – LIFE, WORKS & WRITING OF RIZAL

About Bayaning 3rd World

Bayaning 3rd World (2000), directed by Mike De Leon, is a thought-provoking mockumentary that explores the life, legacy, and identity of José Rizal. Rather than being a conventional biopic, it follows two filmmakers on an investigative journey as they question Rizal’s heroism, his relationship with Josephine Bracken, and the authenticity of his alleged retraction of writings against Spanish friars. The film challenges traditional historical narratives by emphasizing the difficulty of uncovering absolute truth.

Guide Questions & Answers

1. What challenges do the filmmakers anticipate in portraying José Rizal’s life?

The filmmakers anticipate significant challenges due to the multifaceted nature of Rizal’s legacy. They highlight the difficulty of reconciling dramatic storytelling with historical accuracy and the challenge of presenting a figure whose life is shrouded in myth and conflicting testimonies. the film-makers wanted to fill in the ambiguity by creating these characters in their heads of what they should be like and act like, instead they incidentally made biases of the real people they wish to portray, creating more ambiguity. (00:10:15)

2. What are the main arguments regarding the authenticity of Rizal's retraction document?

The film juxtaposes two views: one argues that the retraction was a manipulated document aimed at repairing Rizal’s public image, while the other emphasizes inconsistencies in historical records that cast doubt on its authenticity. (00:22:45)

3. How is Josephine Bracken’s relationship with Rizal depicted in the film?

The film portrays Josephine Bracken ambiguously. Although she is acknowledged as Rizal’s love interest, her role is minimized and depicted as secondary to the larger myth-making process, reflecting how personal relationships are often sidelined in favor of a heroic narrative. (00:30:10)

4. What evidence is provided by the historian regarding Rizal’s alleged retraction?

Evidence includes conflicting testimonies and discrepancies in historical records, with the historian emphasizing that these inconsistencies raise doubts about the document's authenticity. (00:40:05)

5. How do the different portrayals of Rizal’s execution differ, and what do these differences suggest?

The film contrasts a dramatic, sanctified depiction of Rizal’s execution with a more ironic, deconstructed version. This duality suggests that Rizal’s legacy is complex and that historical truth is subject to multiple interpretations. (00:50:30)

6. What reasoning is given to challenge Rizal's heroism?

A character argues that Rizal’s intellectual and somewhat passive nature contradicts the dynamic qualities expected of a true hero, suggesting that real heroism should involve bold, decisive actions. (01:00:20)

7. How does the film depict Rizal’s stance on faith and the Catholic Church?

Rizal is depicted as a conflicted figure who critically engages with the Catholic Church while still acknowledging its cultural influence, reflecting the tension between personal belief and institutional power. Rizal was not against faith itself but was strongly opposed to the abuses and corruption of the friars. (01:10:05)

8. How does the use of fictionalized conversations with Rizal affect the storytelling?

This narrative technique introduces irony and self-reflexivity, blurring the boundaries between historical fact and creative interpretation while prompting viewers to question the nature of historical truth. By using these "characters" as a medium to bridge the gap between fact and subjective perception, the film highlights this ambiguity in a scene where the filmmakers speak with Rizal, asking if the Jesuits told the truth. When Rizal responds, "I did what I had to do," it reinforces the idea that the version of Rizal being interrogated is merely a placeholder for the unresolved questions we continue to ask after his death. (01:22:37)

9. What specific issue causes frustration in the filmmakers’ research on Rizal?

Their frustration arises from the persistent ambiguity in historical records—especially the conflicting accounts of Rizal’s alleged retraction—which makes it nearly impossible to form a definitive narrative. (01:30:45)

10. How do the filmmakers interpret Rizal’s impact on contemporary Philippine society?

They conclude that Rizal’s legacy is dynamic and continuously evolving. His impact endures as a symbol that provokes debate and reexamination of Filipino identity, underscoring his relevance in modern society. (01:40:30)
